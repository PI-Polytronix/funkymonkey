Strategy Built for Long-Term Manufacturing Programs. In aerospace and industrial markets, products and programs last a long time. We make manufacturing partners do the same.

How We Think About Acquisitions

Our acquisition strategy is focused on long-term ownership of specialized manufacturing businesses where continuity, quality, and operational discipline matter. We acquire companies to keep them operating, investing in their people, systems, and infrastructure so they can continue supporting customers reliably for years to come.

When we evaluate a potential acquisition, we focus on whether the business can continue supporting customers reliably through an ownership transition. Our criteria are shaped by what matters most in long-lifecycle manufacturing environments: continuity, quality, and disciplined operations. We look for businesses that are already doing the right things and would benefit from long-term ownership rather than short-term change. The sections below outline the characteristics we typically see in businesses that are a good fit.

We focus on stability and long-term ownership; therefore, we go deep into due diligence.

Acquisition Criteria

We look for established manufacturing businesses that fit most of the following characteristics.

Business Profile

  • Established operating history
  • Recurring customers or long-running programs
  • Profitable or close to profitable
  • Straightforward, understandable operations
  • Manufacturing-focused with existing production, quality systems, and customer approvals
  • United States-based operations, or businesses with a significant U.S. footprint

Business Operation

  • Strong pride in workmanship and product quality
  • Engineering-driven teams solving technical and complex problems
  • Emphasis on doing things the right way, not the fast way
  • A culture where customers and long-term programs come first

Ownership Situations

  • Businesses needing stability and support after ownership or management disruption
  • Specialized manufacturers facing retirement or limited succession planning
  • Owner-led companies where personal constraints limit long-term planning and investment

Ownership Approach

  • Businesses continue operating as they do today
  • Existing leadership and employees are encouraged to remain
  • Company identity and customer relationships are preserved
  • Investment focused on quality systems, compliance, and capacity
  • Decisions made with a long-term operating mindset

    • We prioritize clarity, continuity, and long-term alignment over growth targets.
